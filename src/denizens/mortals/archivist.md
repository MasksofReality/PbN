*Historians, Scholars, and the Keepers of What Is Known*

## Who They Are

In a world where most pre-Sundering knowledge was destroyed or lost, the people who preserve, organize, and transmit what remains are performing an act of survival, not bureaucracy. The gaps are significant: the Lost Decade has no record, the century before the Sundering is fragmentary, and the caravan years exist mostly in oral accounts Archivists have spent two centuries trying to verify. They're also, inevitably, the people who notice when the official record doesn't match other evidence — and most learn not to ask too loudly. The ones who do tend to end up in interesting situations.

## Roleplaying the Archivist

Archivists are perhaps the Mundane Profession most naturally positioned to encounter the supernatural truth — not through violence or chance but through accumulated evidence the official record can't accommodate. A meticulous Archivist who takes their methodology seriously will eventually notice people who appear across generations without aging, events with no mundane explanation, an entire decade with no record. The question isn't whether they find the anomalies. It's what they do when they do.
