## The Wandering Isle

**TL;DR:** *A tropical island that **teleports around the globe.** Easy to reach, nightmarishly hard to leave (the **Governor’s Toll** warps space at its edges). Its wild interior behaves like a pocket dimension. The only real city is **Basilica Purgatory**, ruled in three tiers: the god-like **Governor**, the **Triune** beneath them, and the day-to-day **Council of Six.***

### **Overview**

### The Wandering Isle appeared as an unreachable mist-shrouded “ghost island”early in the Third Era. After a century wandering the world as an enigma, its fog lifted and a being called **The Governor** declared it “open for business”.

**The Governor’s Toll:** Space warps around the coast so severely that a traveler covers only *inches per day* through a mile-thick zone: you must “pay a tithe in time or magic”to leave. Attempt to leave by mundane means while the island *blinks* across the world, and you vanish forever. This once made it the perfect exile for the too-powerful-to-kill (hence the city-island’s name, **Purgatory**): until **waygates** were invented about ten years ago.

### **Geography: The Outer Reaches**

Stubbornly tropical no matter where it lands: Pristine beaches rising into the **Mistcrown Peaks**, wrapped in bioluminescent jungle dotted with dread-soaked ruins that match no known architecture. 

Notable areas:

* **The Raptor Coast** *(east)*: Black volcanic sand patrolled by coordinated, feathered theropod aberrants known for their shadow teleportation ability. They are said to be controlled by a feathered serpentine predator-spirit claiming to be **Quetzalcoatl**. Raptor ichor renders into unusually pure leystone: a lethal but lucrative hunt.

* **Bitterthorn Valley**: Home to a plant-and-crystal dragon-spirit and source of the **Bitterthorn** plant. Tended by hobgoblin **Briarfolk** who trade seeds only with the faetouched; attackers become fertilizer.

* **The Mangrove Coast** *(north)*: A tidal labyrinth where people go to *disappear.* Home to Stilt-hamlets of reclusive Nightfolk and rugged (mildly insane) mortal hermits. The channels belong to drowning **will-o’-the-wisps** and quieter things they lead you toward.

* **Sparklebeard Cove**: Landfall of **Captain Sparkle Beard Von Fancypants**, a Wyrdborn Gentry who plays pirate. Her ramshackle pirate town appears when she’s in residence and melts to mist when she sails: the Isle’s standing welcome for adventure-hungry Lost.

* **The Siren’s Coast** *(northwest)*: Domain of **Amaia, Mistress of the Sea** (of the Triune) and her court of drowned-and-returned “sirens.”Cold but not cruel to the invited; the reef is paved with the bones of everyone else. Work the wrecks here only with her leave, ears wax-stopped.

* **The Ruby Coast** *(southwest)*: Reefs of blood-red **heartglass** (a leystone cousin so potent a hand’s-length feeds a Lesser Heartstone for a *year*). Coveted by every faction, held under Triune control. But heartglass grows *toward the living*: linger too long and you’re found grown quietly into the reef: red, smiling, and caught eternally between living and dead.

* **The Tides of Plenty** *(southern bay)*: Officially the **Bay of Purgatory**; abundant fishing. Holds the **City-Island of Purgatory**, the only true settlement on the whole Isle.

### **Geography: The Inner Reaches**

The interior is a mystery unto itself. The Mistcrown fog now clings to the center. The land is **impossibly large**: What should be a three-day trek has repeatedly defeated multi-month expeditions. Many believe it’s a dimensional rift or a tiny pocket-dimension, uncrossable in human spans of time.

* **The Mistcrown Peaks**: A *threshold*, not a mountain range. Parties that set out together arrive at different ruins inexplicably in the same location, keep mismatched time, and describe the same valley in ways impossible to reconcile—as though the interior rearranges itself when observed. Sooner or later, all report the sense of being **noticed** by a vast, patient attention. No two journeys have ever crossed the same interior twice, and no one has produced a map.

* **The Governor’s Manor**: A quasi-mythic place at the dead center of the Inner Reaches where the Governor is said to dwell, and to which the Isle stays anchored while the rest wanders. No expedition has reached it and returned. The Triune won’t discuss it.

### **Governance of the Wandering Isle**

Three tiers of authority:

1. **The Governor**: Rules the greater Isle with reality-warping power and rare decrees. No one knows who or *what* they are (a Tuatha elder god? a God-Machine servant? the Isle's own *genius loci*?). They communicate only through missives to the Triune, and appear to both control the mists and choose where the Isle teleports.

2. **The Triune**: Three powerful Fae Gentry (godlings of land, sky, and sea) charged with oversight, focused mainly on Purgatory itself. Mostly hands-off: they step in only to break a Council deadlock (as a **tiebreaking 7th vote**) or to grant permission to act in the wilds.

3. **The Council of Six**: The most active power, governing the City-Isle of Purgatory. Five seats go to the leaders of the **five Grand Factions**; the last to a Vampire whose permanent council seat comes from a claim over what became the City-Isle being acknowledged by the Governor.

### **The Triune**

Very little can be said about these three. They are believed to be powerful Fae of the Wyrdborn Gentry granted oversight of the Wandering Isle’s land, sky, and sea by the Governor themselves. 

#### *The bana’Shee*

Originally this entity was the Governor’s sole overseer across the Wandering Isle. The bana’Shee displeased the Governor through a dark bargain he struck with an infernal being so much they wound back time to undo it. As punishment, the bana’Shee was demoted from Lord of the Wandering Isle, to merely Lord of the Wandering Skies.

#### *Amaia*

When the bana’Shee was demoted, his original title was shattered and from it Amaia gained her role as Lady of the Wandering Tides. Though she spends much of her time in her submerged palace of coral and marble off the coast of the Siren Coast, she is a regular enough presence in Purgatory. Though as mercurial as the sea itself and known to eat humans, the Order of Vigiles turn a blind eye to her proclivities—Whether this is out of respect or fear for her vast power is something no one has been bold enough to ask.

#### *The Mallocat*

The being known as the Mallocat is a seemingly whimsical and carefree soul. Though the most disarmingly friendly of the Triune to be sure, those who know them better know this being is a shapeshifter many times older than the Bana’shee and Amaia combined.
