## Who They Are

A Bystander is a Hunter who has forsaken their Creed, abandoned their cell, or lost their supernatural abilities through betrayal, trauma, or a broken vow. They are rare, and considered genuinely dangerous by all sides — not because they retain great power, but because they know exactly how the Hunter community operates, exactly what its weaknesses are, and exactly why they walked away from it.

Every Bystander's story is one of loss. Some lost their Conviction entirely and simply stopped being able to access what they once could. Some were cast out for violating their mandate too seriously to be forgiven. Some walked away deliberately, having decided the cost of the work was higher than they were willing to keep paying. Whatever the cause, a Bystander occupies a genuinely uncomfortable space: they remember what it was to have purpose and ability, and they live now without either, in a world that hasn't gotten any safer just because they've stepped back from defending it.

## Application Requirements

A Bystander Rare Application must explain, in detail:

- What broke the character's faith, cost them their abilities, or ended their standing with their Creed and cell
- Why they remain in Basilica Purgatory rather than leaving for somewhere with no reminders of what they lost
- Whether their former cell, Creed, or the Society of Leopold's oversight structure still has any interest in them — and if so, what kind
- What, if anything, they are trying to reclaim, atone for, or move past

## Mechanical Notes

| | |
|---|---|
| Conviction | Begins at 0. Can be rebuilt through play, but starts from nothing — the character has genuinely lost what they had. |
| Creed Abilities | None accessible at creation. A Bystander who rebuilds Conviction may eventually requalify for their original Creed with Mentor and Admin approval — a significant character arc, not a quick fix. |
| Retained Knowledge | Full knowledge of Hunter tactics, Creed structures, and (if applicable) specific supernatural information they gathered during active service. This makes them valuable and dangerous to their former community in equal measure. |
| Universal Hunter Strengths | Awareness and Will of Iron (see the Hunter overview) are retained — these are tied to the God-Machine's original awakening, not current Conviction, and do not fade with a Bystander's fall. |
| The Booklet | A Bystander's Creed booklet, if they still have it, no longer responds the way it once did — a heavy, silent reminder rather than a functioning artifact. |

## Roleplaying the Bystander

The most compelling Bystander characters have a genuine, specific reason for their fall — not vague burnout, but an actual event, betrayal, or choice that a Storyteller can build story around. Bystanders sit at an unusually rich intersection: still capable of recognizing supernatural activity, still known (and sometimes still wanted) by their former community, but without the resources or standing that made them effective before. Whether they're trying to earn their way back, actively avoiding it, or building something entirely new is one of the most character-defining open questions available in Purgatory by Night.
